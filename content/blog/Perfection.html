Samsung didn’t go big this time on the battery after the Note7 fiasco. But 3000 mAh is more than enough to handle your daily tasks. An average on screen time of 4 hours was recorded doing light tasks at 85-90% brightness. Samsung have also added a wider screen size without increasing the actual phone size. They did that by removing the area that used to contain the home button and stretching down the screen. So, users can get a much more immersed experience; which makes the screen the clearest and sharpest out there. The screen is larger than the iPhone 7 plus but generally the phone is smaller in size. Samsung as two models for this latest flagship phone and it’s not like the previous installments namely: the S6 and S7 with one phone having a normal display and another phone having an edge display, hence the name edge. Both models have edged screens, but they differ in size.  The models are S8 and S8+.
Why didn’t the Samsung Galaxy S7 have a USB type C connecter instead of the traditional micro USB it shipped with, when almost all its competitors that year offered USB-C connections on their flagships?  Well there are three underlying reasons, one is Samsung Gear VR which has a micro USB port and would be incompatible with the Samsung Galaxy S7; this would mean that people who already bought the Gear VR in anticipation of using it with the Samsung Galaxy S7 would be disappointed. The second reason was that Samsung believed that the market was not ready yet and they were right to a degree, since most existing technology was running either micro or mini USB. The third and final reason Samsung offered was that USB fast charging was already extremely effective and Samsung believed that USB-C would be a waste. Finally, Samsung has caught up with other flagship phones. Its connector is now type-C. But this is not the only feature in the new phone. It also has a new AI assistant called Bixby. Samsung claims that the new assistant is a combination of Siri and Google Assistant. Bixby has not been welcomed with open arms with Samsung users in Northern America who almost entirely prefer Google Assistant. The Samsung Galaxy S8 incorporates a dedicated button on its side to activate the digital assistant Bixby. When certain S8 users reprogrammed the button to instead activate Google Assistant Samsung released a patch that prohibited such actions. Is it better than the competition? Contemporarily, the Google Assistant is better in my honest opinion, but Bixby has hope. However, Bixby has the advantage internationally specifically Asian users. You can still use Google Assistant on your Galaxy S8 if you so choose, but give Bixby a chance before you dismiss it.
Another new feature of the new phone is that the finger print sensor is now on the back. This may be good news for people who have been using LG and nexus phones and who want to upgrade to Samsung. But to others the finger print is too close to the camera and causes for an awkward experience. Most reviewers claim it only takes a few weeks to get used to the unorthodox positioning; nevertheless we are obligated to inform you that your camera lens will be smudged now and again.  Samsung has also added other security mechanisms like the Iris scanner. The iris scanner on the Samsung galaxy S8 is a fast and accurate means of security; it first debuted on the Samsung galaxy Note 7 and was initially met with appraisal. The scanner is arguably far superior to face recognition. But despite the fact that Samsung claims the security the scanner offers is nearly on par with fingerprint sensors the Chaos computer club, Europe’s largest association of hackers, has proved that the scanner can indeed be fooled. The CCC has shown that the scanner can be fooled with a shot taken of a person’s eyes with a camera that has a 200 mm lens from a distance of 15 feet away. After taking the shot all that’s left is printing the picture on a typical paper and add a few wet artificial lens to mimic a real person’s eye and you are gold. Although we do not condone, in any form of fashion, invading a person’s privacy by intruding into their phones, but it is possible with the iris scanner in the Galaxy S8. The iris scanner is however coupled with the fingerprint sensor and is a welcome addition to your security, even though it has its flaws.


 My take on the Samsung Galaxy S8  
The Samsung galaxy S8 is without a doubt the most beautiful phone on the market right now. But you should really ask yourselves ‘Is it worth it’? Especially considering the price. The phone costs around 850$ and there are other mid-range phones that are well equipped to perform your desired tasks. Also, there other phones with specs similar to the S8, but at much lower prices like the one plus 5. If you own an S7, my advice would be to stick with your S7 because the S8 does not offer a new extraordinary feature that would demand an upgrade. I would like to point at that the new AI assistant might sound cool but it’s not. Bixby is still in its early stages and is not as polished as Google Assistant or other competitors out there. If you are the type of person who doesn’t mind paying the 850$ MSRP, then go right ahead. In conclusion I will say this The Samsung Galaxy S8 is a beauty to behold.
